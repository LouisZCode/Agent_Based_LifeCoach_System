4.1 CONCEPTS TO CLARIFY / DEEPEN

- Self-compassion vs. self-indulgence: Luis may believe self-compassion means giving up on growth. Clarify this distinction.
- Perfectionism as a protection mechanism: His perfectionism likely started as a way to earn love/approval. Understanding this can soften the pattern.
- The connection between enjoyment and learning: Revisit how relaxation actually improves performance (he understood this intellectually last session)

4.2 SUGGESTED TOOLS & EXERCISES

PRACTICAL SELF-COMPASSION TOOLS:
1. **The Friend Script** - Have Luis practice saying his critical thoughts out loud as if to a friend, then reframe them with compassion. Make it tangible and usable.

2. **The Pause & Redirect** - When perfectionism shows up (during piano or work), teach Luis a simple pause technique:
   - Notice the critical thought
   - Ask: "Is this helping me or hurting me?"
   - Choose a kinder version
   
3. **The Affirmation Anchor** - Build on the homework affirmation. What affirmations resonate with him? What would his younger self need to hear?

4. **Values Check-In** - Help him reconnect with WHY he wanted piano (creative outlet, calmness). When perfectionism creeps in, anchor back to his values, not his performance.

POTENTIAL PARENT EXPLORATION:
When you sense he's ready (likely mid-to-late session), gently explore:
- "You mentioned your parents were disappointed when you didn't do well. What did you learn about yourself from that?"
- "What would it mean if you failed at piano, or failed at work?"
- "What did you need from your parents back then that you're now trying to give yourself through perfectionism?"

This sets up for deeper exploration in future sessions, but don't force it - follow his readiness.
